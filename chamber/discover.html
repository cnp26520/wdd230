Celebrate Diversity

Richmond is home to many different ethnic and religious groups, and that diversity is shown in the city. Home to these different faiths, Richmond has a plethora of churches, cathedrals, synagogues, and more.

A Rich History

Established in 1737, Richmond has been in the United states through almost all its existence, providing valuable insights all the way from colonial life to modern history.

A Sprawling Metropolis

Despite what may seem like an initially low population of 226,604, the greater Richmond area is actually home to more than 1.34 million inhabitants.

Higher Education

Richmond is home to two great universities Virginia Commonwealth University and University of Richmond, who between the two bring in almost 25,000 students every year.

Rife with attracitons

Richmond is home to many popular landmarks and attractions to visit, whether you want to catch a Richmond Flying Squirrels baseball game, visit the holocaust museum, or learn more at the Edgar Allen Poe museum there is always something to do!

Life by the River

Flowing by Richmond is the magnificent James River on which the city was built. Perfect for anyone looking to find some relief from the heat, go for a swim, take a canoe trip, and so much more!
